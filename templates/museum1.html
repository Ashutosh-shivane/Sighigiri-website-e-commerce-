Siddhagiri Gramjivan Museum

        The biggest attraction of the Siddhagiri Matha is the Siddhagiri Museum. It is both a closed and open air museum which depicts village life as it was until 100 years ago, Much of that life has either already disappeared or is disappearing. The idea behind the museum is to preserve the old way of life, and the style of living for the posterity to know. Swamiji realized that the self sufficient way of life that was prevalent just a hundred years ago was a thing of the past. There was no coordinated effort to record this way of life for future generations to know. Swamiji decided to make a museum which show- cased a model self sufficient Indian village. Moreover the earning of the museum would also help in the building of the hospital. With great endeavurs and creative genius of Swamiji the first phase of Museum completed in July 2006. The thought behind museum was to preserve the rural culture and information of Indian way of life. The museum became massively popular among visitors and now it earns around 50.000 to 300.000 rupees daily. Siddhagiri Museum is unique in the way that it depicts the holistic life of Indian village.
